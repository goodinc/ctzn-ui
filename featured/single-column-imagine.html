    Folks were so psyched to have their polaroid (such a novelty) taken to attach to their #DareToImagine postcards to NYC Mayor DeBlasio. They postcard started off - "Dear Mayor DeBlasio We DARE TO IMAGINE a future where homeless folks are..." and folks filled out the rest. Picture the Homeless brough together over 70 people in a public plaza in East Harlem that is "ground zero" for the homeless rights fight to watch a powerful film about Marcus Moore, a homeless leader with PTH, hear live performances by Not 4 Prophet and Rebel Diaz and.... to create these powerful postcards!

    Dare to Imagine in Sacramento CA, Sunday October 18, 2015. We had a mountain of arts and crafts supplies and invited all to create something great. The children were asked, " What do you want to be when you grow up? Some responses: a "real" soccer player, a cop, a chef, a skydiver, another soccer player, an army guy, I'm going to be President! When the children were asked "Can you see into the future? Some responses: no war, no criminals, I'm going to own my own restaurant, I'm going to be a teacher... What a noble lot they are! Thank you for allowing me to participate in this adventure. Lisa Beard

  10. @indiraallegra

    October 20, 2015

    I'm excited to be partnering with YBCA as an emmissary from the US Dept of Art and Culture usdac.us on "Imagination Station / Echolalia: Calling in a More Just and Vibrant World 20 Years from Now" #socialpractice #art #poetry #bodyastext #daretoimagine #usadc See description below: YBCA partners with local violence prevention and youth development organization United Playaz and artist Indira Allegra to create audiopoems with TL and SoMa residents. Together we will create a soundscape of desires for the future of Market Street. Echolalia is a participant of the U.S. Department of Arts and Culture national initiative to dispatch “Emissaries from the Future” to createpop-up Imagination Stations in public spaces nationwide. Through a range of creative activities, Emissaries will engage passersby in exercising social imagination, envisioning the world we wish to inhabit and—looking back from the future—celebrating the work we did to bring it into being. The resulting texts, images, videos, and more will all be uploaded to an online platform, yielding a crowd-sourced vision of the future, inspiring art, policy, and community action.
